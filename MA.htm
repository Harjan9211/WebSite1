M.A.

The M.A. is typically a "taught" postgraduate degree, involving lectures, examination, and a short dissertation.  The Master of Arts in Theology (M.A. in Theology) is a traditional academic graduate degree which consists in the study of theology, focuses upon a specific area such as dogmatic, historical, moral, sacramental, or scriptural, and normally requires the writing and defense of a thesis. In addition to the M.A., there are other master's-level and professional master's degrees dealing with or related to theology.Taught master's programmes involve 2 years of full-time study.
